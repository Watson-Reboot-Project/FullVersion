The age of Moore’s Law – exponentially increasing transistor density in integrated circuits achieved by reductions in transistor size – will soon come to an end due to the underlying physics. However, based on the technologies described in , such as 3-D chip stacking and hybrid photonic/electronic components, I think it is a safe bet that exponential doubling of computing speeds and memory capacity for a fixed dollar will continue roughly at the rate of once every two years through at least 2030.
